Classic Nigerian Meat Pie

⏱️ 45 Mins🍽️ 12 Pies⭐⭐ Medium

Meatpie is a popular, savory Nigerian snack, enjoyed with rich filling of meat and vegetables. Every delightful bite is packed with flavory blends that leave you begging for more!

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 250g butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup cold water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Seasoning cube
  • 1/2 tsp of baking powder
  • Filling: Minced meat, diced potatoes, carrots, onions, curry, thyme, salt.

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, baking powder, salt, and butter in a bowl until it looks like breadcrumbs.
  2. Add beaten eggs and cold water gradually to form a firm dough. Refrigerate for 30 mins.
  3. Boil the meat, and vegetables with spices until tender. Let it cool.
  4. Roll out the dough, cut into circles, and place in pie tins.
  5. Add the filling, cover with another dough circle, and seal the edges with a fork.
  6. Arrange neatly on a baking tray.
  7. Brush with egg wash and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30-35 mins until golden.
  8. Best served piping hot!

Crunchy Chin Chin

️⏱️40 Mins🍽️ Large Batch⭐ Easy

Get ready for the ultimate crunch! This iconic Nigerian snack is golden-fried perfection, packed with a rich, milky flavor and a warm hint of nutmeg. It is the perfect bite-sized party treat that is impossible to put down once you start snapping.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 100g butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp nutmeg

Instructions

  1. Combine flour, sugar, baking powder and nutmeg together.
  2. Add butter and mix.
  3. Add egs and milk and knead to form a dough
  4. Roll out the dough and cut into small cubes or strips.
  5. Deep fry in hot oil on medium heat until golden brown and crunchy.
  6. Drain on paper towels and let it cool completely.

Soft Puff Puff

⏱️ 1.5 Hours (with rising)🍽️ 20+ Balls⭐⭐ Medium

Meet Nigeria’s favorite comfort food. These golden, yeast-risen dough balls are delightfully crisp on the outside and cloud-soft on the inside. Every pillowy bite delivers a gentle, airy sweetness that instantly satisfies your cravings and keeps you hooked.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 cups warm water

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, sugar, yeast, nutmeg, and salt in a large bowl.
  2. Gradually add warm water and whisk until you get a smooth, slightly sticky batter.
  3. Cover and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour until doubled in size.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pot. Use your fingers or a spoon to drop small balls of batter into the hot oil.
  5. Fry until golden brown on all sides. Drain and serve warm!

Instructions

Savory Fish Pie

⏱️ 50 Mins🍽️ 10 Pies⭐⭐ Medium

Dive into a wave of savory perfection with our golden, flaky Fish Pie. This classic pastry is generously packed with a seasoned, moist fish filling and a rich blend of veggies. It is the ultimate comforting treat for seafood lovers looking for a delicious, savory escape.Butter Pie

Ingredients

  • 4 cups flour
  • 250g butter
  • 1/2 cup cold water
  • Fish or pepper sauce
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Filling: Boneless fish (shredded tin mackerel or sardines), boiled eggs, onions, curry, thyme, scotch bonnet pepper.

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, baking powder, salt, and butter in a bowl until it looks like breadcrumbs.
  2. Add beaten eggs and cold water gradually to form a firm dough. Refrigerate for 30 mins.
  3. Mix the shredded fish with diced eggs, peppers, onions, sauce and spices.
  4. Roll out dough, fill with the fish mixture, and seal tightly.
  5. Brush with egg wash and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30 mins until golden.

Crunchy Plantain Chips

⏱️ 25 Mins🍽️ Large Batch⭐ Easy

Savour the irresistible crunch of this legendary Nigerian street favorite! Made from perfectly sliced plantains and fried to a flawless golden crisp, it is the ultimate sweet-and-savory snack.

Ingredients

  • 4 unripe (green) plantains
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: Pepper or garlic powder

Instructions

  1. Peel the green plantains and slice them very thinly (use a mandoline if you have one).
  2. Soak plantain slices in cold water and salt for 10 minutes. This helps give the plaintain a unique crispy texture when frying.
  3. Seive out the water from the plantain and sprinkle some salt, peper and garlic powder over it
  4. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  5. Fry the slices in batches until they turn golden and crispy.
  6. Remove, drain on paper towels, and sprinkle with salt while hot.

Homemade Potato Chips

⏱️ 20 Mins🍽️ Large Batch⭐ Easy

Potato chips are not just crispy, each crunch is a savory journey into a wild blend of hot salty spice that leaves your tongue with that irresistable and unforgettable. It is one of Nigerian's favorite quick snack.

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: white pepper or chicken seasoning for flavor

Instructions

  1. Peel potatoes and slice them into very thin rounds.
  2. Soak in a bowl cold water for 30 mins to remove starch.
  3. Drain and rinse under cold water, then spread on a kitchen towel and pat to dry completely
  4. Stir fry in hot oil(170C/340F) until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Drain and season immediately with pepper or chicken seasoning.
